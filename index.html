CS 3130 Teaching Assistant - Spring 2025 to Present

For the past two semesters, I have been an assistant to Professor Charles Reiss at the University of Virginia for his Computer Systems and Organization 2 course. I hold labs and office hours to aid in instruction and homework help, covering various topics such as virtual memory, parallel processing and threading, and pipelines all in C programming language.

This position has challeneged my communication skills more than any role I've held, and I've developed a lot in that respect since I was hired. I chose to become a TA for this course after I struggled with it immensely, since I wanted to gain a further understanding for the extremely interesting content, especially virtual memory and side-channel attacks, the topics that interesting me the most.

Research Assistant - Spring 2025 to Present

With Robbie Hott, as my mentor, I have been taking part in the AI Smart-Classroom Initative (ASCI) project that has been running for years at UVA. My part in it has been developing a local LLM tool to summarize and highlight student issues to prime them for more effective and efficient office hours. This is part of a research goal to discover if AI, and more specifically LLMs, can be used to improve the efficiency of helping students in large class sizes.

Junior Software Developer for ATC - Summer 2025 to Present
